Do any of the following apply to you?

  • I am insolvent
  • I am an undischarged bankrupt
  • I have a receiving order on my property
  • I've had a penalty for late filing of my tax affairs within the past 5 years
Help with this question
  • you are considered insolvent if you owe more then £5,000 to a creditor and you're unable to pay your debts
  • you are an undischarged bankrupt if your bankruptcy is ongoing and you remain subject to the payments and restrictions that go along with the bankruptcy process
  • A receiving order on your property means a court has placed your property under the control of the official receiver or of a trustee in bankruptcy
  • you can get a late filing penalty if your tax return is more than 3 months late
